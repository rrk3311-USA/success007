How Sclera White Works: The Science Behind Eye Whitening

The Mechanism of Action

Sclera White operates through multiple complementary pathways to enhance eye appearance and health:

The formula delivers powerful antioxidants like Lutein, Zeaxanthin, Vitamin C, Vitamin E, and Alpha Lipoic Acid that neutralize free radicals and oxidative stress in eye tissues. This protection helps maintain the clarity and whiteness of the sclera while supporting overall ocular integrity.

2. Blue Light Filtration
Lutein and Zeaxanthin accumulate in the macula and lens, where they act as natural filters for harmful blue light from screens and digital devices. By reducing blue light damage, these carotenoids help prevent eye strain, redness, and the dullness that comes from excessive screen exposure.

3. Circulatory Support
Ingredients like Bilberry Extract and Grape Extract support healthy microcirculation in the delicate blood vessels of the eyes. Improved circulation means better nutrient delivery and waste removal, which contributes to clearer, brighter-looking eyes.

4. Anti-Inflammatory Action
Eyebright, Quercetin, and Rutin provide natural anti-inflammatory support, helping to reduce redness and irritation that can make eyes appear dull or discolored.

5. Cellular Energy and Repair
B-vitamins, L-Taurine, and supportive minerals like Zinc and Selenium fuel cellular energy production and repair mechanisms within eye tissues, promoting long-term ocular health and appearance.

The Science of Eye Appearance: Why Eyes Lose Their Whiteness

Common Causes of Yellowing and Dullness

1. Oxidative Stress
Free radicals from UV light, pollution, and metabolic processes damage proteins in the sclera, causing yellowing similar to aging paper.

2. Poor Circulation
Sluggish microcirculation leads to waste accumulation and reduced nutrient delivery to eye tissues.

3. Inflammation
Chronic low-grade inflammation from allergies, dryness, or irritants causes persistent redness.

4. Blue Light Exposure
Digital devices generate oxidative stress in eye tissues through prolonged blue light exposure.

5. Dehydration
Insufficient water intake affects tear film quality and eye surface health.

6. Nutritional Deficiencies
Lack of specific nutrients like Lutein, Vitamin A, and antioxidants compromises eye health.

7. Sleep Deprivation
Poor sleep prevents natural eye rejuvenation and repair processes.

8. Aging
Natural collagen breakdown and cumulative oxidative damage affect scleral appearance.
