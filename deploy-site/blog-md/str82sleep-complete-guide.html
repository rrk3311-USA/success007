Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I take Str82sleep every night long-term?

A: Yes, Str82sleep is formulated for regular use. The ingredients have long safety histories. However, it's worth periodically assessing if you still need nightly support or if improved sleep hygiene allows less frequent use.

Q: Will I feel groggy in the morning?

A: Most users wake feeling refreshed, not groggy. If you experience morning drowsiness, you may be taking the supplement too late, too close to wake time (needs 7-8 hour sleep window), or you may be sensitive to the dose.

Q: How is this different from prescription sleep medications?

A: Str82sleep works with your body's natural sleep mechanisms rather than forcing sedation. It's non-habit forming, supports healthy sleep architecture, and doesn't cause rebound insomnia when you stop using it.

Q: Can I take this with my antidepressants?

A: Consult your psychiatrist or prescriber. Some interactions are possible, particularly with SSRIs and melatonin. However, many people safely combine them under medical supervision.

Q: What if I wake up in the middle of the night?

A: Str82sleep is designed to support sleep maintenance, but if you wake, avoid taking additional doses. Instead, practice sleep hygiene techniques: avoid looking at clock, keep room dark, practice relaxation breathing.

Q: Is 5mg of melatonin too much?

A: 5mg is a moderate dose. Some people do well with less (0.5-3mg), others need more. Research shows 5mg is effective for most people without significant side effects. Start with full dose as formulated; adjust only if needed.

Q: Can this help with jet lag?

A: Yes! Melatonin is well-established for jet lag recovery. Take at target bedtime in new time zone to help reset circadian rhythm. Ashwagandha also helps body adapt to stress of travel.

Q: Will I become dependent on this?

A: No. These ingredients are not habit-forming. Some people develop psychological dependence on any sleep aid, but there's no physiological withdrawal from stopping Str82sleep.

Q: Can I take this if I'm pregnant?

A: Consult your obstetrician. While individual ingredients like melatonin have some research in pregnancy, comprehensive advice from your doctor is essential.

Q: Does this work the first night?

A: Many people notice improved sleep onset the first night from melatonin. However, full benefits from adaptogenic and calming herbs build over 2-4 weeks of consistent use.
